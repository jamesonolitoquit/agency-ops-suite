
# Service Agreement (Template)

This Service Agreement ("Agreement") is between the Client and the Agency ("Provider"). Use this template as a starting point—have legal counsel review before use.

1. Services
- Provider will deliver the services described in the attached Statement of Work (SOW). The SOW defines deliverables, timelines, revisions, acceptance criteria, and any third-party costs.

2. Fees & Payment
- Client will pay the fees specified in the SOW. Unless otherwise stated, payments are due according to the SOW schedule.
- Standard rule: 50% upfront, remaining balance due before final delivery/launch. Recurring services (hosting, maintenance) are billed separately and in advance.
- Late payments may incur interest and Provider may suspend services after 15 days past due.

3. Revisions & Scope
- The SOW lists included revision rounds. Additional work outside the SOW is change-order work billed at the agreed hourly rate and will require a signed change order.

4. Timeline & Delays
- Provider will use commercially reasonable efforts to meet timelines. Delays caused by Client (late approvals, missed deadlines, missing assets) may shift delivery dates and may incur additional costs.

5. Intellectual Property
- Upon full payment, Provider assigns to Client the IP rights for deliverables created under this Agreement, excluding Provider pre-existing materials and third-party licensed components.
- Provider retains the right to display deliverables in portfolios and marketing materials unless Client requests an embargo in writing.

6. Hosting, Third-Party Services & Maintenance
- Hosting, domain, and third-party service fees are Client's responsibility unless included in the SOW. Provider will assist with integrations and account setup as agreed.

7. Warranties & Liability
- Provider warrants that services will be performed with reasonable skill and care. Except where prohibited by law, Provider's total liability is limited to the fees paid for the services under this Agreement in the prior three months.

8. Termination
- Either party may terminate for material breach if uncured after 30 days' written notice. On termination, Client pays for work performed through the termination date. Provider will deliver completed work and assist in handover upon final payment.

9. Confidentiality
- Each party will keep confidential information private and use it only to perform obligations under this Agreement.

10. Acceptance
- Deliverables are considered accepted after Client's written approval, or after 7 days without written objection following delivery, whichever occurs first.

11. Governing Law
- This Agreement is governed by the laws of the jurisdiction specified in the SOW.
